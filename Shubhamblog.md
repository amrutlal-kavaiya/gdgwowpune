# My Journey with Google Developer Groups on Campus

## How I Joined GDG on Campus

When I first heard about the Google Developer Group (GDG) coming to our campus, I was both excited and nervous. As a student passionate about technology, I had always followed Google's innovations and developer tools from afar, but I never had the opportunity to be part of a community that focused on these technologies.

The announcement came through our college's technical club newsletter - GDG WOW (Women of Web) Pune was organizing an introductory session on campus. Though I wasn't sure what to expect, I registered immediately, hoping to learn more about Google technologies and possibly meet like-minded peers.

## The Unforgettable Introductory Day

The introductory day turned out to be one of the most inspiring experiences of my academic journey. I arrived early at the venue, watching as the room gradually filled with students from various departments, all sharing the same curiosity and enthusiasm.

### Warm Welcome and Community Spirit

From the moment I stepped in, the GDG organizers created an atmosphere that was welcoming and inclusive. They greeted everyone personally and encouraged us to introduce ourselves to fellow attendees. What struck me most was how they emphasized that GDG wasn't just about technical expertise but about community, collaboration, and growth.

### Eye-Opening Sessions

The day began with an overview of what GDG is globally and specifically what GDG WOW Pune aims to achieve. The speakers shared their personal journeys in tech, which were relatable and inspiring. They covered:

- The range of Google technologies and platforms
- How GDG provides resources and mentorship to learn these technologies
- Opportunities for networking and professional growth
- Upcoming workshops, hackathons, and events

What made these sessions special was that they weren't just theoretical - there were quick hands-on demonstrations that gave us a taste of what future workshops would offer.

### Interactive Workshops

The highlight of the day was a mini-workshop on Google Cloud basics. What I expected to be a complex topic was broken down into understandable components, and by the end of the session, I had actually deployed my first small application on Google Cloud Platform! The sense of achievement was incredible.

### Building Connections

During lunch and coffee breaks, I connected with students from other colleges and departments. We shared our interests, challenges, and aspirations. Some were beginners like me, while others had more experience but were equally enthusiastic about learning together.

The GDG mentors circulated among us, answering questions and providing guidance. I was amazed at how approachable they were - no question was considered too basic or unimportant.

## Why This Day Was Special

The introductory day was transformative for several reasons:

1. **Breaking Barriers**: It demystified technologies that had seemed intimidating before
2. **Community Connection**: It showed me the power of learning within a supportive community
3. **Practical Learning**: The hands-on approach proved that these skills were attainable
4. **Inspiration**: Hearing success stories from people who started just like me was motivating
5. **Clear Path Forward**: I left with concrete next steps and resources to continue learning

## Looking Forward

Since that introductory day, I've been an active member of GDG WOW Pune. Each event builds upon the warm, collaborative foundation established on that first day. I've participated in workshops, contributed to projects, and even helped organize a few events.

What started as curiosity has evolved into a passion, and I'm grateful that GDG provided that initial spark and continues to fuel my journey in technology.

I would encourage every student, regardless of their background or current skill level, to join their local GDG chapter. The technical knowledge is valuable, but the community, mentorship, and opportunities for growth are truly priceless.
