# GDG WOW Pune 2025: Celebrating Women in Tech

*Published on April 20, 2025 by GDG WOW Pune Team*

![GDG WOW Pune 2025 Banner](https://www.gdg.community.dev/assets/logo.svg)

## Event Overview

The GDG WOW (Women of Wonders) Pune 2025 event was held on April 15-16, 2025, at the Hyatt Regency Pune Hotel & Residences. The two-day conference brought together over 500 tech enthusiasts, industry leaders, and Google Developer Experts to celebrate women in technology and promote diversity and inclusion in the tech industry.

## Theme: "Tech Without Boundaries"

This year's theme, "Tech Without Boundaries," focused on breaking barriers that limit innovation and inclusion in the technology sector. The event highlighted how technology can be leveraged to create solutions that are accessible to all, regardless of gender, background, or abilities.

## Day 1 Highlights

### Opening Keynote

The event kicked off with an inspiring keynote from **Dr. Priya Sharma**, Engineering Director at Google India, who shared her journey in tech and emphasized the importance of creating inclusive spaces for women in technology. Her speech on "Building the Future We Want to See" resonated deeply with the audience and set an energetic tone for the conference.

### Technical Workshops

The first day featured several hands-on workshops covering cutting-edge technologies:

- **AI and Machine Learning Ethics** led by Neha Patil, ML Engineer at Microsoft
- **Cloud-Native Application Development** with Aparna Joshi, Google Cloud Advocate
- **Mobile Development with Flutter** by Sneha Desai, Flutter GDE

The workshops provided participants with practical skills and knowledge that they could immediately apply to their projects.

### Panel Discussion: Breaking Glass Ceilings

A highlight of Day 1 was the panel discussion featuring women leaders from various tech companies discussing challenges and strategies for women advancing in tech careers. The panelists shared personal experiences and actionable advice that inspired many attendees.

## Day 2 Highlights

### Technical Deep Dives

Day 2 focused on deeper technical content with tracks covering:

- **Advanced Web Development** - Progressive Web Apps and Web Assembly
- **Data Science and Analytics** - Practical applications and case studies
- **DevOps and Site Reliability Engineering** - Best practices and tools

### Hackathon Showcase

The "Hack for Inclusion" hackathon teams presented their solutions addressing accessibility and inclusion challenges. The winning team, "TechBridge," developed an AI-powered application that converts sign language to text and speech in real-time, impressing judges with both technical execution and potential impact.

### Closing Keynote

The event concluded with an electrifying talk by **Radha Narayan**, CTO of a leading Indian startup, who discussed "The Future is Collaborative" and emphasized how diverse teams lead to more innovative and successful products.

## Networking and Community Building

Beyond the formal sessions, the event offered numerous opportunities for networking, mentorship, and community building:

- **Speed Mentoring Sessions** - 15-minute one-on-one sessions with industry experts
- **Career Fair** - Representatives from over 20 companies discussed job opportunities
- **Community Showcase** - Local tech communities shared their initiatives and invited new members

## Personal Experience

Attending GDG WOW Pune 2025 was truly a transformative experience. As someone relatively new to the tech industry, I was initially intimidated by the prospect of networking with accomplished professionals. However, the welcoming atmosphere and supportive community quickly put me at ease.

The workshops were exceptionally well-organized, with clear instructions and helpful mentors ready to assist whenever I got stuck. The Flutter workshop was particularly enlightening - I had dabbled in mobile development before, but learning from Sneha Desai, a Google Developer Expert, gave me insights and best practices that would have taken months to discover on my own.

What stood out most was the genuine commitment to inclusion. The event wasn't just about women in tech; it was about creating a tech industry where everyone can thrive. Seeing male allies actively participating in discussions about diversity and inclusion was refreshing and encouraging.

The "Hack for Inclusion" hackathon was intense but incredibly rewarding. Our team of four, who had never worked together before, collaborated on an accessibility solution for visually impaired users navigating public transportation. Although we didn't win, the experience of rapid prototyping and receiving feedback from experts was invaluable.

Perhaps the most impactful aspect was the connections formed. I met peers facing similar challenges, mentors willing to provide guidance, and potential collaborators for future projects. Two weeks after the event, I'm still in touch with several attendees, and we've even started a study group for advanced cloud computing topics.

## Impact and Takeaways

The GDG WOW Pune 2025 event made a significant impact on the local tech community:

- **Skills Development**: Attendees gained practical knowledge and skills in emerging technologies
- **Community Growth**: The event strengthened the local women in tech community with many new members joining GDG Pune
- **Job Opportunities**: Several attendees secured interviews or job offers through connections made at the event
- **Mentorship Relationships**: The event facilitated numerous mentorship connections
