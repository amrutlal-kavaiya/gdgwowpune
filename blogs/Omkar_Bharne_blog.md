# My Journey with GDG WOW Pune

## How I Discovered and Joined GDG WOW

My journey with Google Developer Group Women in Tech (GDG WOW) Pune began quite unexpectedly. I had been following various tech communities in Pune for some time, always on the lookout for opportunities to learn and connect with like-minded individuals. 

It was during a casual scroll through LinkedIn when I noticed a post about an upcoming GDG WOW event in Pune. What caught my attention wasn't just the impressive lineup of speakers and workshops, but the community's commitment to creating an inclusive environment for everyone interested in technology, regardless of gender or experience level.

Although "Women in Tech" was in the name, I learned that GDG WOW welcomes people of all genders who support the mission of bringing more diversity to the tech industry. This inclusive approach immediately resonated with me, as I strongly believe in the importance of diverse perspectives in technology.

After researching more about GDG WOW Pune, I registered for the event through their community page. The registration process was straightforward, and I received a confirmation email with all the details about the upcoming event. The organizers also added me to a WhatsApp group where participants could interact before the event, which helped break the ice.

## My First Day Experience

The first day of the GDG WOW event exceeded all my expectations. The event was held at a modern tech hub in Pune, with a vibrant atmosphere that was buzzing with energy from the moment I arrived.

### The Welcome

Upon arrival, I was greeted by friendly volunteers who helped with the check-in process. Each participant received a welcome kit that included a name tag, event schedule, some cool GDG stickers, and a notebook. What impressed me was how the organizers made an effort to make everyone feel welcome, especially first-time attendees like myself.

### Opening Ceremony

The event kicked off with an inspiring opening ceremony. The GDG WOW Pune organizers introduced themselves and shared the community's vision and goals. They emphasized that the event was not just about learning technical skills but also about building connections and supporting each other in our tech journeys.

### Keynote Session

The highlight of the morning was undoubtedly the keynote session delivered by a prominent woman leader from Google. She shared insights about emerging technologies and her personal journey in the tech industry. The session was not only informative but also deeply motivational, addressing challenges faced by underrepresented groups in tech and strategies to overcome them.

### Interactive Workshops

The day continued with parallel workshop tracks covering a variety of technologies including Android development, web technologies, cloud computing, and AI/ML. I chose to attend a hands-on workshop on Flutter, which had been on my learning list for months.

The workshop was expertly facilitated, with a perfect balance of theory and practical exercises. What stood out was how the facilitators catered to different skill levels, ensuring that both beginners and more experienced developers could learn something valuable.

### Networking Lunch

During lunch, the organizers had arranged informal networking tables based on different interests and technologies. This thoughtful arrangement made it easy to find people with similar interests and strike up meaningful conversations. I connected with several developers and even found potential collaborators for a project idea I had been contemplating.

### Panel Discussion

The afternoon featured a panel discussion on "Building an Inclusive Tech Ecosystem." The diverse panel included industry leaders, community organizers, and advocates who shared practical insights on creating more inclusive workplaces and communities. The discussion was followed by an engaging Q&A session where participants could ask questions and share their experiences.

### Hands-on Coding Session

The day concluded with a collaborative coding session where participants worked in small groups to solve challenges using Google technologies. This practical approach to learning was refreshing and helped reinforce concepts covered earlier in the day.

## What Made It Special

What truly made my first day at GDG WOW Pune special was the sense of community. Unlike other tech events I've attended, there was a genuine emphasis on collaboration rather than competition. More experienced developers were helping newcomers, ideas were flowing freely, and everyone seemed invested in each other's growth.

The organizers had clearly put thought into creating an environment where learning could happen naturally, through both structured sessions and informal interactions. The diversity of participants – in terms of gender, experience level, and technical backgrounds – contributed to rich discussions and multiple perspectives on every topic.

## Looking Forward

As I left the venue after the first day, I felt energized and inspired. I had learned new technical skills, made meaningful connections, and found a community that aligned with my values. The event had successfully bridged the gap between technical learning and community building.

I'm looking forward to remaining an active member of the GDG WOW Pune community, attending future events, and perhaps even contributing as a speaker or volunteer in the future. This first day experience has convinced me that being part of such a community is invaluable for anyone looking to grow in the tech industry.

If you're considering joining GDG WOW or attending one of their events, I wholeheartedly recommend it. Whether you're a seasoned developer or just starting your tech journey, you'll find value in the knowledge shared and connections made.
