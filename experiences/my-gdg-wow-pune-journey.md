# My Journey at GDG WOW Pune 2025

## Introduction

I had the incredible opportunity to attend the Google Developer Group Women in Tech (GDG WOW) event in Pune this year. This write-up captures my experience, the knowledge gained, and the connections made during this transformative event.

## Day 1: Workshops and Hands-on Sessions

### Morning: Welcome and Keynote
The day began with a warm welcome from the GDG Pune organizers who outlined the agenda for the two-day event. The opening keynote by a Google Developer Expert on "The Future of AI in Everyday Applications" set an inspiring tone for the day.

### Technical Workshops
I participated in two hands-on workshops:
- **Flutter Development Workshop**: Learned how to build cross-platform applications using Flutter, implementing Firebase authentication, and creating beautiful UI components.
- **Cloud ML Workshop**: Got hands-on experience with Google Cloud's AutoML tools to create a simple image classification model without extensive coding.

### Networking Lunch
The lunch break provided a great opportunity to connect with fellow developers and exchange ideas. I met professionals from various backgrounds, including mobile development, data science, and cloud architecture.

## Day 2: Speaker Sessions and Panel Discussions

### Morning: Technical Talks
The second day featured technical talks covering:
- "Building Scalable Applications with Google Cloud Platform"
- "Women in Open Source: Pathways to Contribution"
- "Implementing Accessibility in Mobile Applications"

### Afternoon: Career Panel
The highlight of day two was the "Women in Tech Leadership" panel discussion featuring industry leaders sharing their career journeys, challenges, and advice for emerging professionals.

### Hackathon Showcase
The event concluded with presentations from teams who participated in the pre-event hackathon focused on sustainable development solutions. The innovative ideas and implementations were truly inspiring.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Technical Growth**: Enhanced my skills in Flutter development and gained practical experience with ML tools.
2. **Community Connection**: Made valuable connections with like-minded professionals and potential mentors.
3. **Inspiration**: The stories shared by women leaders in tech offered practical strategies for navigating challenges in the industry.
4. **Resources**: Discovered numerous learning resources, open-source projects, and local tech communities to continue my growth journey.

## Impact on My Career

Attending GDG WOW Pune has significantly impacted my professional outlook. The technical workshops have directly enhanced my skill set, while the networking opportunities have expanded my professional circle. The advice from established women in tech has given me new perspectives on career growth and overcoming obstacles.

## Conclusion

The GDG WOW Pune event was much more than just technical sessions – it was a holistic experience that addressed the multifaceted aspects of being a woman in technology. I'm grateful for the opportunity to have been part of this empowering community event and look forward to applying the knowledge and connections gained to further my career and contribute to the tech ecosystem.
