# My Experience: AI/ML/RAG Session at GDG WOW Pune

## Event Overview

I had the incredible opportunity to attend the AI/ML/RAG (Retrieval Augmented Generation) session organized by GDG Women of Web Pune. The event brought together technology enthusiasts, AI practitioners, and curious minds for an insightful deep dive into the world of artificial intelligence and machine learning, with a special focus on RAG technologies.

## Key Learnings

### Understanding RAG (Retrieval Augmented Generation)

The session provided a comprehensive overview of RAG, a powerful approach that enhances large language models by incorporating external knowledge retrieval. I learned that:

- RAG combines the generative capabilities of LLMs with information retrieval systems
- It significantly reduces hallucinations by grounding responses in retrieved context
- The architecture typically involves embedding documents, storing vectors, and retrieving relevant context during inference

### Practical Applications of RAG

I was fascinated by the diverse applications of RAG technology across industries:

- Customer support systems that can access up-to-date product documentation
- Legal research tools that can cite specific precedents and regulations
- Enterprise knowledge management that leverages internal documents and resources
- Educational platforms that can provide contextually relevant learning materials

### Hands-on Implementation

The workshop portion of the session was particularly valuable, where we:

1. Built a simple RAG system using open-source tools
2. Explored document chunking and embedding strategies
3. Implemented vector search using different similarity measures
4. Evaluated the performance of our RAG system against baseline LLM outputs
