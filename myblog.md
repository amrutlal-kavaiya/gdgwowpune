# My GDG WOW Pune Experience

## Introduction

I had the incredible opportunity to attend the Google Developer Group (GDG) Women of Wonders (WOW) event in Pune this past weekend. This was my first time attending a tech community event specifically focused on women in technology, and I'm excited to share my experience and takeaways.

## The Event

The GDG WOW Pune event was held at the Hyatt Regency on April 15, 2025. The venue was perfect for networking and collaborative sessions, with ample space for workshops and presentations. The event was well-organized with a thoughtful schedule that balanced technical content with opportunities for connection.

### Highlights

#### Opening Keynote: "Breaking Barriers in Tech"
The day began with an inspiring keynote from Priya Sharma, a Senior Engineering Director at Google. She shared her 15-year journey in tech, highlighting both challenges and triumphs as a woman in engineering leadership. Her emphasis on finding mentors and building a supportive community resonated strongly with attendees.

#### Technical Workshops
I participated in two hands-on workshops:
1. **Building with TensorFlow for Responsible AI** - This session provided practical guidance on implementing ethical AI principles in machine learning projects. We worked through examples of bias detection and mitigation techniques.
2. **Flutter Development for Cross-Platform Applications** - A comprehensive introduction to Flutter that walked us through building a functional app in just 90 minutes!

#### Panel Discussion: "Navigating Career Growth"
A diverse panel of women in various tech roles discussed strategies for career advancement, work-life integration, and overcoming imposter syndrome. The candid sharing of personal experiences created a powerful sense of solidarity.

#### Lightning Talks
Several community members delivered 5-minute presentations on projects they're passionate about. These ranged from open-source contributions to innovative applications of emerging technologies.

## Personal Takeaways

### Technical Growth
The event significantly expanded my technical knowledge, particularly in machine learning ethics and Flutter development. I left with practical skills I can immediately apply to my projects.

### Community Connection
Perhaps the most valuable aspect was connecting with other women in tech who face similar challenges and share similar aspirations. I exchanged contact information with several attendees and have already scheduled coffee chats to continue our conversations.

### Inspiration for Contribution
The event motivated me to become more active in the tech community. I'm planning to:
- Attend regular GDG Pune meetups
- Prepare a talk for an upcoming event
- Mentor junior developers in my area of expertise

## Conclusion

The GDG WOW Pune event exceeded my expectations in every way. Beyond the technical content, the sense of community and empowerment was palpable. Events like these play a crucial role in increasing diversity in tech by providing education, inspiration, and connection.

I'm grateful to the organizers and volunteers who made this event possible, and I'm already looking forward to next year's GDG WOW!
